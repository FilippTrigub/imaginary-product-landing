# Quantum Physics — A Summary

> Research compiled on February 9, 2026, from multiple web sources.

## What Is Quantum Physics?

Quantum physics is the study of matter and energy at the most fundamental level — the realm of atoms, subatomic particles, and photons. It aims to uncover the properties and behaviors of the very building blocks of nature.

## Core Principles

### 1. Quantization of Energy

Energy exists only at discrete values — tiny packets called **quanta**. This revolutionary idea was introduced by Max Planck in 1900 and laid the foundation for all of quantum theory.

### 2. Wave-Particle Duality

Matter and light exhibit properties of both particles and waves. Albert Einstein's 1905 explanation of the photoelectric effect demonstrated that light is made of particles called **photons**, earning him the Nobel Prize in 1921.

### 3. Superposition

A quantum system can exist in multiple states simultaneously until it is measured. Only upon observation does the system "collapse" into a single definite state.

### 4. Quantum Entanglement

Two particles can become linked such that the state of one instantly influences the state of the other, regardless of the distance separating them. Einstein famously called this *"spooky action at a distance."*

### 5. Heisenberg's Uncertainty Principle

It is fundamentally impossible to simultaneously know both the exact position and exact momentum of a particle. The more precisely one property is measured, the less precisely the other can be known.

## Modern Understanding (2025–2026)

Modern quantum mechanics has evolved from depicting electrons as particles orbiting nuclei to understanding them as distributed within **orbitals** — mathematical probability distributions describing where electrons are likely to be found. Research continues to push boundaries in areas like quantum gravity and the connection between quantum mechanics and space-time.

## Real-World Applications

Despite its counterintuitive nature, quantum physics underpins much of modern technology:

- **Lasers** — used in communications, medicine, and manufacturing
- **Transistors & semiconductors** — the backbone of all modern electronics
- **MRI machines** — medical imaging powered by quantum spin properties
- **Quantum computing** — an emerging field promising exponential computational power

## Why It Matters

Quantum mechanics is widely regarded as the most successful theory in the history of physics. It explains phenomena ranging from why the sun shines to the iridescent colors of butterfly wings. While quantum effects act on every scale, they are most apparent at the atomic and subatomic level — yet they govern reality at its deepest layer.

The field continues to open doors into mystery, paradox, and awe, while simultaneously driving the most cutting-edge technological innovations of our time.
