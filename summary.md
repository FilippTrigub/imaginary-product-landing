# Quantum Physics Summary

Quantum physics (quantum mechanics) describes the behavior of matter and energy at atomic and subatomic scales. Its key principles include wave-particle duality, where particles exhibit both wave-like and particle-like properties; quantization of energy levels; and the probabilistic nature of particle positions and momenta described by the Schr√∂dinger equation. Quantum entanglement allows particles to exhibit correlated states regardless of distance, challenging classical intuitions about locality. Applications span semiconductors, lasers, MRI technology, and emerging quantum computing.
